about me

About me

I grew up in Ostia, the neighbourhood of Rome (Italy) near the seaside. I obtained by Bachelor's degree in Physics in 2020 from Sapienza University of Rome. I moved to Munich, Germany, where I received my Master of Science in Physics at the Ludwig-Maximilian University of Munich (LMU) in 2022. 

Currently, I am a PhD student at the European Southern Observatory (ESO) in Garching, close to Munich, Germany.​ I am passionate about atmospheres, and I conduct research in studying the Earth as an exoplanet and exoplanet atmospheres.

I am also a Student Representative at ESO and part of the Diversity, Equity and Inclusion (DE&I) Committee of ESO. In my free time, I love travelling, being outside in the nature and playing basketball!

My Research

My work is at the interplace between Exoplanet Atmospheres and Atmospheric Physics.

My publications are on NASA ADS and Google Scholar. 
Observing experience at the VLT with FORS2 in Paranal.
PI of an awared proposal to characterise an exoplanet atmosphere, co-PI of other proposals.
Experience in using satellite data from ERA5 to MODIS land surface product (MCD43D, Version 6.1)

Atmospheric physics

I sudy Earth's global albedo and cloud properties using state-of-the-art satellite and reanalysis products.

exoplanet atmospheres

I study the Earth as an exoplanet through Earthshine observations.

Earthshine observations

The Earth as an exoplanet

I perform 3D radiative transfer simulations to understand the spectral and temporal variability of Earth-like exoplanets.
My research particularly focuses on the effect of clouds and different land surfaces, which are related to the habitability of the planet.

Community & Outreach

As one of the Student Representative at ESO, we try to improve the conditions and the working environment of PhD students, while being a point of contact among students and the Office for Science. Among my roles, I have been welcoming the new students at ESO (July 2023 - February 2024), I organised a Mental Health Awerness event for Students and Fellows (February 2024), and I have been handling disagreements and controversies among the student community, with the help of respectful workplace advisors.

In addition, I have been very active in coordinating all Student Reps activities and communicating with the Office for Science. Together with the Office of Science, we applied for funding to organise three PhD courses over 2024 on topics chosen by the students via surveys. I have also been a co-organiser of the Social Day (October 2023) for the Directorate of Science.

I am very pruod of having implemented an informal discussion series on the topics of Diversity, Equity and Inclusion for ESO Students, in collaboration with Francesca Primas. 
I am very greatful for my all other Student Reps for their help and for being such a great team! 

In May 2024, I have been part of the ESO Student Selection Committee, and since September 2023 I have been one of the co-organizer of the topical meeting "Stellar Coffee and Planeraty Tea" meeting with an ESO Fellow (before with Loiuse Nielsen, and later with Jens Kammerer). I also served as Scientific Assistant for the Observing Programmes Committee (OPC) and I helped with the Astronomy for non-Astronomers talks.

I also had the great opportunity and responsability to be the first Student Representative in the Diversity, Equity and Inclusion Committee of ESO. Though this experience, I got exposed on the many problems and initiatives of the Committee, and this made me grew a lot as a person. I helped co-organising talks, writing announcements in the Internal News and being a reference person for the student community on these topics.

Summer Research program, I ran student elections and several surveys among students

I am very passionate about doing outreach activities, in particular to stimulate interest and being a role model for young girls and other minorities. I represented ESO at the European Patent Office (EPO) Girls' Day in Munich (April 2024), where I explained my path in becoming a Researcher and an Astrophysicist. Before, I have been one of the co-developer of the Pale Blue Dot project, which aims at inspiring your kids about the uniqueness of our planet from an astrophysical perspective. I have done many outreach talks in school, both in Italy and in Germany.
